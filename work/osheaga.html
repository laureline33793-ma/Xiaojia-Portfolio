Info

Osheaga is an incredible 3-day music and arts festival hosted on a beautiful island in Montréal, bringing together tens of thousands of music lovers every summer.

The goal of this project was to capture that massive, natural and vibrant energy and channel it into a cohesive brand identity—starting with a custom wordmark logo and expanding into an event brochure, a responsive website, and everything in between.

The Challenge

Managing a 3-day festival with 6 different stages means dealing with a massive puzzle of schedules, artist lineups, and locations.

The real test was bringing this complex lineup to life across totally different mediums—making it easy to read on a printed brochure, a desktop website, and a mobile screen, all while keeping the festival’s unique visual identity consistent through deep research.

What I Learned

Reflecting on this project, I’ve identified several areas for growth. I realized that I tended to work in a bubble because I was hesitant to reach out for feedback, which limited the perspectives I could have gained. Moving forward, I want to be more open to communication to grow as a designer. I also struggled with my workflow; my files were quite unorganized, which slowed me down significantly when putting this Process Book together. I am now committed to developing better file management habits for large project as part of my professional practice. Despite these challenges, this project taught me the importance of a solid foundation. In the beginning, spending so much time on the black-and-white wordmark felt tedious since I couldn’t use colors or graphics yet. However, I soon realized that refining those core details was worth the effort, as the wordmark is the most frequent element across all materials. I also gained a much better understanding of typography across platforms. By printing out tests and studying brand guidelines, I learned how to adjust font scales so they look right on everything from a large screen to a mobile app and physical brochures. I plan to continue my deep dive into typography, as it truly is the soul of design.

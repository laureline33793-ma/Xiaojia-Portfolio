Info

Trout Lake Little League is a long-running community baseball program in East Vancouver. This rebranding project refreshes its visual identity for families, coaches, and young players while honoring the league’s history since 1953.

The work spans logo design, illustration, print collateral, merchandise, and a responsive website concept that makes registration and community updates easier to find.

The Problem

The existing brand felt fragmented across signage, social posts, and volunteer-made materials. Parents struggled to recognize official communications, and the digital experience did not reflect the warmth of the in-person community.

We needed a flexible system that works on the field, in print, and on phones, without losing the neighborhood character that keeps families coming back each season.

What I Learned

Working on this brand identity project has been a journey of significant growth. In the beginning, we moved slowly because we were stuck on how to combine hand-drawn elements with a modern aesthetic. We eventually realized they aren’t contradictory: the hand-drawn style provides the brand’s warmth, while the modern design acts as the structural framework. I also learned that establishing a solid brand identity system must come before creating any assets; without that foundation, it’s easy to lose our way. On a personal level, this large-scale project taught me the vital importance of organization. My messy files and folders slowed me down significantly, making me realize that clear file naming and a structured Google Drive are essential for efficiency. Finally, I learned that while brainstorming is great, you eventually have to narrow down your keywords and moodboard inspirations to commit to a direction rather than staying in a state of constant expansion.
